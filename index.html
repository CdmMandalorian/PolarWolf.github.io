The Polar Wolf

The Artic Wolf Cub

Roaming freely, in the North American tundra, a small batch containg anywhere from 5 to 7 pups are born. Breed from only the Alpha male and Beta female from the pack; these newborns, are brown in color. Unable to open their eyes for the initial 10 days, they are protected by the whole pack. The mother being ultra protective, will not allow other pack members into the mother's den; till the pups are 2 weeks old. The father wolf is in charge of raising the pups, using playfull means to teach them to hunt. At six weeks they grow strong enough to travel, and by then will join the rest of the pack. Quite interestinly enough, all pups are born with blue eyes; but later change tone to either a gold or brown color.

White Wolves

By the time, the pup joins the pack; their coat of fur evolves pearly white. Traveling, and learning survival skills, roaming through the polar ice that is North America. Due to the mast size, and freedoom that is within their enviroment, the White Wolf, is the only wolf sub-species that is not threatened, by any habitat destruction. The Artic wolf hardly ever comes into human contact, as they reside higher than the Northern Tree Line. As the winter season approaches, these wolves grow a second layer of fur, to combat the tundra cold.

The Polar Effect

As their prey line lays the most active, during the winter season, these animals do not hibernate. A nice "cool" mechanism, that is similiar to feet of ducks and penguins; is that it maintains their paws at a temperature lower than thier body core. This component, minimizes heat loss at their paws eventhough they are traveling through the frozen tundra. As the blood floows into their paws it heats the blood that is leaving, preventing their core from ever getting cooled by the loss of heat through their feet.
